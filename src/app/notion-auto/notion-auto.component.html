Notion Automation

Cutting information into digestible amount

The challenge

A block of information

Every week during my studies at southern cross university a module was given to me taking the form of a lesson on one long web-page.
It was difficult for me to retain every information when I had to read the whole document at once, and I ususally forgot the important parts when I had to use them on the next class.
These modules were too much information for me all at once.

The idea

Dividing the information block

After identifying that the problem was the size of the information to digest at once, my idea was to reduce this amount by dividing it.
I had made a Notion space for this project, writing ideas and linking resources of existing systems. That is when I thought, why not using Notion to organize these division of modules.
I had already experiences in working with Notion and knew that automation tools existed, making it possible.

The project

Automate the division and Notion storage

Searching for automation tools I found n8n, a source-available free tool if self-hosted, offering everything I needed.
As accessing the module information from the university needed identification, my way of getting it would first be by hand, copying and pasting the block in a Notion database page.
That is when n8n would see a new page, read it and every time a title element appears, create a new sub-page in the database with the title and the text corresponding below. As simple as that.

The add-ons

Calendars reminders, Ai generated questions

After successfully dividing the module in sub-pages easy to read, my next goal was to create reminders in my calendar linked to the sub-pages so that I could read the element in regular times along the week.
I had to divide the number of days hours in the week by the amount of subpages and just link my calendar through n8n to easily add them to my notifications.
Next was questions, the goal is to have questions about what I just read at the end of the sub-page so that I can verify that I understood the lesson.
Using the text of the sub-page and a prompt, I was able to ask chatGPT for 5 questions related simply with n8n node, then adding them at the end of the sub-page.

The deliverable

Digestible content, AI-generated questions and calendar reminders

I decided to keep the end product as a locally hosted system that I could turn on and off manually as I want to keep control over its functionnalities.
The final stage of the product consists of division of the original module in sub-pages, with 5 AI-generated questions added at the end of each sub-page about its content. When created, each sub-page is linked to an event in my calendar allowing me to read every part of the module little by little every day until the lecture.
I am happy about this project as I managed to make it work for my second term of studies after designing it throughout my first term, and I am using it everyday, even on my 5th term now.
